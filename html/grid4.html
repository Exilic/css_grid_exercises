Reference to the world

Words refer to the world in different degrees of specificity. To refer to a person as »Elizabeth II« or »the Queen« differs in degrees of specificity. The latter reference depends to a higher degree on context to gain specific reference to the world. Talking in the United Kingdom about »the Queen« will connect the recipient of the words with Elizabeth II. Doing the same in Denmark will connect the recipient of the words with Margrethe II. A word of much less specificity, »she,« will gain specificity in a sentence if it follows directly after a sentence containing the name »Elizabeth II.« Some sentences later, the word »she« will follow another name and the specific reference of »she« will have changed. A different kind of speech situation, but with a similar function, would be a person pointing at a picture of Elizabeth II and saying »she.«
